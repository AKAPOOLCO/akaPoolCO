📋 Standard Operating Procedure
1 · Daily Route Operations ▼
Beginning the day
Open the Route tab. Pools scheduled for today are listed. Drag using the ☰ icon to match your driving order.
⚡ Quick Visit — maintenance only
For mid-week checks or bi-weekly stops. Tap ⚡ Quick.
→Empty skimmer, brush, OTO chlorine presence test.
→Logs the visit and proves presence. Does not alter the pool's chemical baseline data.
🧪 Full Log — chemical workup
Tap 🧪 Full Log. Perform a Taylor K-2006-SALT titration test.
→Input exact readings. App calculates CSI and dosing in oz/lbs based on pool volume and SWG status.
→Check work boxes. Tap Submit & Email Client.
2 · Chemistry Targets (TFP Standards) ▼
Targets are set per pool in the Pool Profile — never globally — to account for equipment and environment differences.
Traditional liquid chlorine pools
→CYA Target: 40 ppm
→FC Target: 6.0 ppm (~15% of CYA for Granite Bay summer heat)
Saltwater generator (SWG) pools
→CYA Target: 70 ppm — protects SWG cell from overworking
→Salt Target: 3200 ppm — standard for Pentair/Hayward
→Liquid chlorine dosing is suppressed for SWG pools unless FC drops below 2.0 ppm
3 · Predictive Engine — Z-Score Logic ▼
Every pool has a unique "appetite" for chlorine based on its size, sun exposure, and vegetation. Instead of reacting to low chlorine, akaPoolCO tracks each pool's exact Chlorine Demand Rate in ppm/day.
How the math works
The app calculates each pool's historical average demand (mean μ) and natural fluctuation (standard deviation σ). On each new test a Z-score is computed:
Z = (X − μ) / σ
X = today's demand rate · μ = pool's historical average · σ = standard deviation
What the alert means
A ⚠️ High Demand badge appears when Z > 2.0 — meaning the pool is consuming chlorine at a rate statistically far beyond its normal behavior. Two likely causes:
1Organic contamination — an algae bloom is forming invisibly, or high organic load from bathers, fertilizer runoff, or debris.
2Equipment failure — the SWG cell is calcifying or failing and no longer producing its baseline chlorine.
Statistical tracking catches unseen problems weeks before water turns cloudy or a salt cell burns out.
Minimum data required
Z-score activates after 4 full FAS-DPD logs per pool. The app shows a countdown in the dosing panel on each visit until the baseline is established.
